Exercises

After each class, work in groups of 2 or 3 to produce an RMarkdown document in which you answer the questions below. Knit your document into a .pdf file and submit it to the Moodle discussion forum for feedback. For each question, explain and justify your approach and conclude briefly.

Here is an example of header for your RMarkdown document:

---
title: "Homework #1"
author: "Group: the kurtotic avengers"
date: "2019-10-23"
output:
  pdf_document:
    fig_caption: no
    number_sections: no
    toc: yes
    toc_depth: 2
---

For the pdf output to work, you need TeX installed on your computer. On a Mac, try MacTeX, on a PC, see options here. If you cannot get a pdf output, try exporting as Word or html (change pdf_document to word_document or html_document in the header), then print the html document as pdf.

They found larger mean brain volumes in men than in women (look at the supplementary information to see how they used some of the shift function code to illllustrate the effects!). They also found increased variance in the sample of men relative to the sample of women. The means and standard deviations of total brain volumes for men and women reported by Ritchie et al. are available in the next chunk.

Use at least nsim=1000 iterations. To check your code, start with nsim=100.

To compare variances, a modified percentile bootstrap test is implemented in comvar2(). The syntax is comvar2(x,y,nboot=1000). The function returns several outputs, including a confidence interval, but no P value. You can source the function using the next chunk:
